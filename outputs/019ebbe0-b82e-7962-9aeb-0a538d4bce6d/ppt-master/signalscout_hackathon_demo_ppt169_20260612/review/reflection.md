# Hackathon Demo Pitch Reflection

## Fit

The deck is suitable for a 3 to 5 minute slide section before the live demo. It follows the requested narrative: short setup, product framing, product proof, commercial readiness, then direct transition into the running website.

## Strengths

- The story is buyer-facing: public job postings become buying signals, then ranked leads, evidence, outreach, and Slack handoff.
- The deck does not over-explain the live demo. Slide 08 makes the handoff explicit, so the product remains the proof object.
- Fresh screenshots from the local app are used on the cover and product proof slide, grounding the pitch in the actual build.
- The post-review pass removed internal source labels and reframed proof around one concrete outcome: signal feed to ranked account to seller handoff.
- Speaker notes keep the slide portion paced and avoid claims like guaranteed purchase intent.

## Watchouts

- The presenter should keep slides 06 and 07 concise. If those run long, the live demo will lose time.
- In the live demo, avoid overclaiming integration breadth. Frame the current state as a marketable workflow with demo-safe integrations.

## Recommendation

Use the deck as-is for the hackathon pitch. Target roughly 4 minutes on slides, then switch immediately into the website demo flow from `docs/DEMO_FLOW.md`.
